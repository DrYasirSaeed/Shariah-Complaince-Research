Research Methodology & Data Construction Note1. Data Source and Sample PeriodThe longitudinal dataset for this study is constructed from the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) publications titled "Financial Statements Analysis of All Listed Non-Financial Companies." * Time Horizon: 2005 – 2023 (19 Years).Scope: All non-financial firms listed on the Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX).2. The Multi-Schema ChallengeA significant methodological hurdle in this research was the evolution of SBP reporting standards. Over two decades, the SBP revised its data hierarchy and formula brackets three times. To maintain longitudinal integrity, this study developed three distinct computational pipelines to map these shifting schemas into a unified master dataset:PeriodSchema CharacteristicsKey Adaptation2005–2008Legacy SBP formatDirect mapping of sub-component sums.2009–2013Transitional formatUpdated variable anchors for non-current liabilities.2014–2023Modern IFRS-aligned formatFuzzy-matching engine to handle variable sub-bracket expansions.3. Variable OperationalizationThe following Shariah-screening and profitability ratios were computed for each firm-year observation:3.1 Profitability MetricReturn on Assets (ROA):$$ROA = \frac{\text{Profit After Tax}}{\text{Total Assets}}$$3.2 Shariah Screening RatiosDebt Ratio (DR):$$DR = \frac{\text{Long-Term Debt} + \text{Short-Term Borrowings}}{\text{Total Assets}}$$Investment Ratio (IR):$$IR = \frac{\text{Short-Term Investments}}{\text{Total Assets}}$$Income Ratio (IncR):$$IncR = \frac{\text{Non-Compliant (Other) Income}}{\text{Total Sales}}$$Illiquid Asset Ratio (IAR):$$IAR = \frac{\text{Non-Current Assets} + \text{Inventories}}{\text{Total Assets}}$$Net Liquid Assets (NLA):$$NLA = \frac{\text{Total Assets} - \text{Illiquid Assets} - \text{Total Liabilities}}{\text{Total Number of Outstanding Shares}}$$4. Computational Logic & Data Cleaning4.1 Fuzzy-Logic Variable MatchingThe SBP often changed the bracketed sub-totals in their reports (e.g., changing "Non-Current Assets (A2+A3)" to "(A1+A3+A4+A5+A6)").Logic: The Python pipeline used a text-search engine to locate the "root" of a variable name (e.g., "Non-Current Assets") rather than relying on static cell references. This ensures the correct total was captured regardless of the sub-component count.4.2 Null-Safe Mathematics ("Safe Addition")In financial reporting, a "blank" cell often represents zero exposure (e.g., a company with no short-term debt). Standard code often treats Number + Null as Null.Logic: A custom safe_add function was used to treat null values as $0$ during additions (Debt + STB) while preserving the null status for divisions to avoid invalid $0/0$ results.4.3 Data NormalizationRaw SBP data contained various formatting inconsistencies:Negative Values: Converted from accounting parentheses (500) to floats -500.0.Percentages: Strings like "5.5%" were stripped and divided by 100.Numeric Cleaning: Commas and non-breaking spaces were removed to ensure all values were valid floating-point numbers.5. Sequence and Hierarchy PreservationUnlike standard database merges that default to alphabetical sorting, this dataset was merged using Categorical Sequencing.Strategy: The original "Sector" and "Sub-Sector" order from the SBP Excel sheets was captured and used as a sorting index.Result: The final dataset maintains the exact economic hierarchy of the Pakistan Stock Exchange, allowing for seamless sector-wise comparative analysis.This methodology ensures that the findings of this thesis are based on a dataset that is mathematically robust and longitudinally consistent.
